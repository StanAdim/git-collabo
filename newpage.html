The South China Sea raid was cond ucted by the United States Third Fleet between 10 and 20 January 1945 during the Pacific War. Undertaken to support the liberation of Luzon in the Philippi nes, it targeted Japanese warships, supply convoys and air craft in the region. After attacking airfields and shipping at Formosa and Luzon, the Third Fleet entered the South China Se a and aircraft from its carriers attacked Japanese shipping off Frenc h Indochina on 12 January, sinking 44 vessels. The fleet then sailed north and attacked Formosa again on 15 January. Further raids wer e conducted against Hong Kong, Canton and Hainan the next day. Th e South China Sea raid was highly successful as the American force de stroyed a large number of Japanese ships and aircraft, while losing relatively few aircraft. According to historians, the destruction of c argo vessels and oil tankers was the most important result of the raid, as t hese contributed to closing a supply route which was vital to the Japanese war
